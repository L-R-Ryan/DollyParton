DOLLY PARTON

Dolly Parton was born in the impoverished Appalachian region of Tennessee in 1946, growing up with 12 siblings. She refers to herself back then as so poor the ants wouldn't even steal from them.

Her childhood home is featured here and on the album cover of her 1973 My Tennessee Mountain Home.

Her rise to fame is undeniably impressive, landing her in many halls of fame, associated with hundreds of songs, receiving over one hundred awards, and donating a vast majority of her wealth to support her community.

Ranging from classic country legend Johnny Cash to pop country hit Luke Bryan, country music casts a wide net. Still, it's sound is unique to the United States. A bubble that doesn't stand out in the presence of Taylor Swift and Elvis Presley is renowned artist Dolly Parton.

Despite her comparatively small impact on the Billboards, she is famous for more than her solo music career. Out of these major players on the Billboards, she has collaborated with 14 of them. This would be higher, as many people wanted to record the infamous song eventually performed by Whitney Huston, I Will Always Love You. Elvis Presley was one of them.

Nominated

Of the 413 awards she has been at least nominated for, she holds Guinness World Records, million-plus streaming awards, countless Country Music Awards, and teaching recognition.

She has been nominated for a Grammy 55 times to date.

She has received awards notably from the Carnegie Corporation of New York, United States Fish and Wildlife Service, Daughters of the American Revolution, Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars of the Smithsonian Institution, United States Library of Congress and the United States Congress.

She has been induced into the Grammy Hall of Fame multiple times, the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, and the Library of Congress's National Recording Registry.

In 1985 Drinkin'stein won the Worst Original Song Award.

Dolly has rejected the Presidential Medal of Freedom twice. Another former president was shocked upon hearing the news, assuming she had received one already.

In 1985 Sweet Lovin' Friends and Rhinestone were nominated for Worst Original Song and Worst Musical Score.

Her long-time musical partner Porter Wagoner has been at least nominated with her as a duo 34 times.

Famous title Jolene recorded in 1973 won the Best Country Duo / Group Performance Grammy with Pentatonix in 2017.

Dolly Parton grew up in an extremely impoverished area of the country. Her father was illiterate and access to medical care good medical care wasn't always available. She has been quiet about her philanthropy, but many of the ones that are publicly known have been focused on trying to help support her home state, particularly in the areas that she observed to be lacking - health and education.

Her biggest impact is probably the purchase and continued expansion of Dollywood, a themepark right in her home county. An assessment by the Tennessee Department of Transportation estimates the economic impact of Dollywood to be ~1.8 billion per year. Dollywood currently employs ~11,000 people. The impacts of a successful tourist attraction in an impoverished region cannot be overstated.

There are certainly many factors that may drive poverty rates, but census data appears to suggest that Sevier County performed better over the decades than surrounding counties, possibly due to the Dolly's repeated attempts to help the region, especially with the support of Dollywood.
